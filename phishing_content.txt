PHISHING AWARENESS TRAINING

1. Introduction
Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source.

2. Common Types of Phishing
- Email Phishing: Fake emails asking users to click links or download attachments
- Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing attack on a specific person or organization
- Smishing: Phishing through SMS messages
- Vishing: Phishing through voice calls

3. How to Identify Phishing
- Suspicious sender email address
- Urgent or threatening language
- Spelling and grammar mistakes
- Unknown links or attachments
- Requests for personal or financial information

4. Example of a Phishing Email
Subject: Urgent! Your bank account will be blocked
Message: Click the link below immediately to verify your account and avoid suspension.

Warning Signs:
- Creates fear and urgency
- Asks to click a suspicious link
- Pretends to be from a bank

5. Prevention Tips
- Do not click unknown links
- Verify sender email addresses
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Keep software updated
- Never share passwords
- Report suspicious emails

6. Conclusion
Phishing attacks are very common, but users can stay safe by being alert, checking links carefully, and avoiding suspicious messages.
